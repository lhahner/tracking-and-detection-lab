% What does the paper contain?
Edozie et. al \cite{edozie} provides a review across the past developments of object detection techniques in the past 26 years. Besides describing the evolution of deep learning based object detection systems they also compare them to the classical non-deep-learning based object detection methods and algorithms.
% Why is this interesting in this report?
They provide a wide overview of datasets, methods and research that was important in the past 26 years. Beside providing classical object detection methods they also present an overview of modern deep learning based object detector which should be summarized here to give an impression of the state of the art.

% What review is provided
Object detection systems are separated in One-Stage and Two-Stage object detectors. Where the two-stage detector first generates proposals of the detection and then encodes them to make class predictions. Otherwise the first-stage detector doesn't consider region proposals, they simply rely on the locations with the most objects and either classify them as an object or background. Through this simplification One-Stage detectors are generally faster than two-stage detectors.

Neha et al. \cite{1} provides an overview of detection systems using deep learning and classical approaches. They provide a summary of datasets, metrics and methods relevant in this domain. Further they provide a list of strengths and weaknesses of the different deep-learning based detection systems and also provide benchmark results on various detection systems, e.g.: R-CNN, Yolo etc.
